# UNICORN AD CREATIVE ENGINE v1.0

## PURPOSE

Landing pages get seconds to convert. **Meta ads get 0.5 seconds.**

The unicorn-conversion-engine validates long-form persuasion. This
engine validates **instant visual impact.**

One question: **Will this ad stop the thumb and compel the click?**

Not "is this a decent concept?" Not "does this follow best
practices?"

Will a distracted human, mid-scroll through vacation photos and
political arguments, stop on THIS image and feel compelled to engage?

If the answer isn't "yes, inevitably," the ad isn't ready.

## TRIGGER CONDITIONS

**Standalone trigger:**

-   "Run unicorn ad creative engine on these concepts"

-   "Validate these ad ideas"

-   "Will these ads stop the scroll?"

-   "Rate these Nano Banana prompts"

**Auto-chain after:**

-   meta-image-ad-generator completes ad concepts

-   persona-driven-ads generates creative ideas

-   brand-style-extractor provides visual guidelines

-   Any ad creative reaches "concept ready" status

## THE THUMB-STOP STANDARD

AD PERFORMANCE PREDICTION SCALE

===============================

1-3: Invisible - thumb scrolls right past without registering

4-5: Wallpaper - registers subconsciously but no pause

6-7: Glance - brief attention, continues scrolling

8: Pause - scroll stops, considers, most continue

9: Stop - involuntary thumb-stop, strong click impulse

10: Magnetic - impossible to scroll past, click is inevitable

MINIMUM DELIVERY THRESHOLD: 9/10

Ads scoring below 9 are not ready for testing budget.

The recursive loop continues until 9+ is achieved.



## THE 10 AD CREATIVE PROTOCOLS

Each protocol pressure-tests a different dimension of ad effectiveness.
All must pass threshold.

### PROTOCOL 1: THUMB-STOP POWER (9/10 threshold)

Does the visual create an involuntary pattern interrupt in under 0.5
seconds?

**Audit Criteria:**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Element**                         **What to Check**
  ----------------------------------- -----------------------------------
  Color Disruption                    Does it contrast against typical
                                      feed content (blues, whites,
                                      greens)?

  Visual Tension                      Is there unexpected composition,
                                      asymmetry, or visual conflict?

  Face Power                          If faces present, are eyes
                                      compelling? Direct gaze? Emotional
                                      expression?

  Motion Implication                  Does a static image suggest
                                      movement, action, or energy?

  Scale Disruption                    Is something unexpectedly large,
                                      small, or out of proportion?

  Pattern Break                       Does it violate visual expectations
                                      in a compelling way?
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Scoring Rubric:**

-   10: Physiologically impossible to scroll past - involuntary stop

-   9: 90%+ of target personas would pause mid-scroll

-   8: Strong attention grab, most would notice

-   7: Noticeable but not commanding

-   6: Blends somewhat with feed

-   \<6: Invisible wallpaper

**Common Failures:**

-   Safe, expected imagery (stock photo aesthetic)

-   Colors that match typical feed content

-   Centered, balanced, "professional" composition

-   No human element or emotional expression

-   Generic product shot without context

### PROTOCOL 2: INSTANT PERSONA RECOGNITION (9/10 threshold)

In 0.3 seconds, does the target persona know "this is for ME"?

**Audit Criteria:**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Element**                         **What to Check**
  ----------------------------------- -----------------------------------
  Identity Markers                    Lifestyle cues, aesthetic
                                      preferences, aspirational signals

  Problem/Desire Signaling            Visual representation of their pain
                                      or dream state

  Demographic Resonance               Age-appropriate styling, relevant
                                      context, familiar environment

  Tribal Belonging                    Symbols, aesthetics, or scenarios
                                      that signal "your people"

  Mirror Moment                       Do they see themselves or their
                                      aspirational self?
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**The 0.3-Second Test:** Before any conscious processing, does the
persona's brain flag this as relevant?

**Scoring Rubric:**

-   10: "This is literally about ME" - feels personally targeted

-   9: Immediate "this is my world" recognition

-   8: Strong relevance signals, clear target

-   7: Generally applicable to broad audience

-   6: Could be for anyone

-   \<6: Wrong audience entirely

**Common Failures:**

-   Generic imagery that applies to everyone (applies to no one)

-   Mismatched lifestyle/aesthetic signals

-   Wrong age/demographic representation

-   Missing environmental or contextual cues

-   Aspirational imagery that feels unreachable vs. attainable

Does the image create an open loop that DEMANDS closure (the click)?

  Outcome Curiosity       "What happened/will    Before state,
                          happen?"               mid-action, implied
                                                  after

  Mechanism Curiosity     "How does this work?" Intriguing process,
                                                  unusual method

  Social Curiosity        "What do others        Group reaction,
                          know?"                 exclusive access
                                                  implied

  Self-Curiosity          "Could this be me?"   Relatable
                                                  transformation,
                                                  quiz/diagnosis
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**The Open Loop Test:** Does viewing this image create TENSION that can
only be resolved by clicking?

**Scoring Rubric:**

-   10: Unbearable curiosity - MUST click to resolve tension

-   9: Strong pull toward resolution - most would click

-   8: Clear curiosity hook - many would click

-   7: Mild interest - some might click

-   6: Slight intrigue - few would click

-   \<6: Complete at glance - no reason to click

**Common Failures:**

-   Image tells the whole story (no reason to click)

-   Curiosity gap is weak or generic

-   Gap is confusing rather than intriguing

-   Too much mystery (frustrating, not compelling)

-   Click wouldn't actually resolve the gap

**Desire Intensity Scale:**

-   10: Overwhelming want - desire is visceral and immediate

-   9: Strong "I need this" response

-   8: Clear desire activation

-   7: Interest in learning more

-   6: Mild curiosity about offer

-   \<6: No desire generated

**Common Failures:**

-   Showing the product instead of the outcome

-   Outcome feels unrealistic or unattainable

-   Desire is intellectual, not emotional

-   Missing the specific desire trigger for this persona

-   Generic benefit visualization

### PROTOCOL 7: BRAND CONSISTENCY (8/10 threshold)

Does the ad feel authentically on-brand while standing out?

**Brand Alignment Audit:**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Element**                         **Check**
  ----------------------------------- -----------------------------------
  Color Palette                       Primary/secondary colors present
                                      and correct

  Typography Style                    Font feeling matches (if text on
                                      image)

  Visual Aesthetic                    Photography style, illustration
                                      style, mood

  Brand Personality                   Tone alignment (playful, premium,
                                      bold, etc.)

  Quality Level                       Production value matches brand
                                      positioning
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**The Balance:**

-   Too branded = boring, expected, invisible in feed

-   Too different = confusing, off-putting, breaks trust

-   Perfect = unmistakably them, but surprisingly fresh

**Scoring Rubric:**

-   10: Perfectly on-brand AND scroll-stopping

-   9: Strong brand consistency with creative edge

-   8: Clearly on-brand, recognizable

-   7: Brand elements present but inconsistent

-   6: Loosely on-brand

-   \<6: Off-brand or completely generic

**Common Failures:**

-   Sacrificing brand for attention (short-term win, long-term loss)

-   So on-brand it's predictable and invisible

-   Brand elements feel forced rather than integrated

-   Mismatched quality level (premium brand, cheap-feeling ad)

### PROTOCOL 8: PLATFORM OPTIMIZATION (8/10 threshold)

Is this optimized for Meta's algorithm and user behavior?

**Format Optimization:**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Placement**           **Optimal Specs**       **Key Considerations**
  ----------------------- ----------------------- -----------------------
  Feed                    1:1 or 4:5              Max real estate, bold
                                                  composition

  Stories/Reels           9:16                    Full screen,
                                                  bottom-safe CTA zone

  Right Column            1:1                     Simple, bold, readable
                                                  at tiny size

  Audience Network        Varies                  Needs to work across
                                                  contexts
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Andromeda Algorithm Alignment:**

-   Does the visual encourage engagement (comments, shares)?

-   Does it avoid creative fatigue patterns?

-   Is it different enough from recent brand creative?

-   Does it work for broad AND narrow targeting?

**Technical Checklist:**

-   Safe zones respected (no critical content in crop zones)

    Text under 20% (or strategic violation)

    Mobile-first composition

    Works at 320px AND 1080px

    High contrast/readability

    Fast visual comprehension

**Scoring Rubric:**

-   10: Optimized for all placements, algorithm-friendly

-   9: Strong platform optimization across primary placements

-   8: Works well on main placements

-   7: Adequate but not optimized

-   6: Some platform violations

-   \<6: Major platform issues

### PROTOCOL 9: COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION (8/10 threshold)

Would this stand out in a feed full of competitor ads?

**Competitive Audit Questions:**

-   What do competitor ads in this category typically look like?

-   What visual conventions are overused?

-   What's the "wallpaper" that everyone ignores?

-   What would be genuinely surprising or different?

-   Where's the white space in visual approach?

**Differentiation Strategies:**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Strategy**                        **Example**
  ----------------------------------- -----------------------------------
  Format Break                        Everyone uses photos, you use
                                      illustration

  Color Inversion                     Category is bright, you go dark (or
                                      vice versa)

  Perspective Shift                   Everyone shows product, you show
                                      outcome

  Emotional Flip                      Everyone is aspirational, you're
                                      validating

  Context Disruption                  Expected context → unexpected
                                      context

  Style Contrast                      Polished category → raw/authentic
                                      (or vice versa)
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Scoring Rubric:**

-   10: Category-defining originality, would change competitor behavior

-   9: Clearly differentiated, memorable, would stand out

-   8: Noticeably different from competitors

-   7: Somewhat unique elements

-   6: Minor differentiation

-   \<6: Blends with competitor creative

**Common Failures:**

-   Following category conventions "because they work"

-   Different for different's sake (weird, not effective)

-   Copying what's currently winning (will fatigue)

-   Ignoring competitor landscape entirely

## PROMPT IMPROVEMENT PROTOCOL

When validating Nano Banana 2 / AI image generation prompts:

FOR EACH PROMPT FAILING VALIDATION:

1\. IDENTIFY failing protocol(s)

2\. DIAGNOSE prompt weakness:

- Missing visual tension/pattern break?

- Weak persona signals?

- No emotional trigger?

- Curiosity gap absent?

- Brand elements missing?

- Generic/category-standard approach?

3\. ENHANCE prompt with specific additions:

THUMB-STOP ENHANCEMENTS:

└── "dramatic lighting" / "bold color contrast"

└── "unexpected composition" / "visual tension"

└── "dynamic angle" / "extreme close-up or wide"

└── "caught mid-action" / "decisive moment"

PERSONA RECOGNITION ENHANCEMENTS:

└── Specific lifestyle markers

└── Environment details matching their world

└── Clothing/style/aesthetic cues

└── Activity or context they identify with

EMOTIONAL TRIGGER ENHANCEMENTS:

└── "expression showing [specific emotion]"

└── "body language conveying [feeling]"

└── "atmosphere of [mood]"

└── "moment of [emotional beat]"

CURIOSITY GAP ENHANCEMENTS:

└── "partial reveal" / "obscured element"

└── "intriguing object" / "mysterious element"

└── "story mid-action" / "implied before/after"

└── "viewer POV" / "breaking fourth wall"

BRAND ALIGNMENT ENHANCEMENTS:

└── Specific color palette injection

└── Brand aesthetic descriptors

└── Quality level indicators

└── Mood/tone alignment language

4\. REVALIDATE improved prompt

5\. ITERATE until 9+ achieved
